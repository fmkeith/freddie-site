The last day was a lighter one — not much building, just a quieter pace to close out the week. The main thing I made was my first game, a simple browser-based FPS, and I'm genuinely proud of it. A few days ago I didn't know what a `div` was, so shipping something you can actually play feels like a big jump.

We went out for lunch in the afternoon and ended up talking mostly about AI. It came up naturally because the whole week had been about building with code, and Stu told me he hasn't really had to write code himself for around two years now — AI tools handle it well enough that he just directs them instead. He said he's actually forgotten a fair amount of the syntax at this point. That was strange to hear from someone who taught me everything I know this week, but it also made sense. The job shifted from writing code to knowing what to ask for and whether the result is right.

He walked me through his current projects — he's doing work for a pharmaceutical company, which sounds like a completely different world from personal sites and games, but the underlying skills are the same. Knowing what problems need solving, understanding the tools, checking the output.

I've picked up a lot this week. How websites actually work, what Git is for, how to deploy something so it runs on a real server, how to read an error message instead of panicking at it. More than the specific things though, I think I've got a clearer idea of how this kind of work actually feels day to day — which is what work experience is supposed to do, I think.
